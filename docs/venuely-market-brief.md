# Venuely — ZA Wedding-Venue SaaS Market Brief

*Blunt advisor mode. Pre-revenue, solo founder, R1499/mo (~USD 80).*

## 1. Market size & shape

There is no clean published count of ZA wedding venues. Triangulating from directories and industry reports:

- **sa-venues.com Cape Winelands**: 13 listed venues — but that's only a curated subset of one sub-region. ([sa-venues](https://www.sa-venues.com/westerncape/weddings/cape-winelands.php))
- **saweddings.co.za** and **Pink Book** list hundreds across Western Cape, Gauteng, KZN.
- StatsSA: ~135–160k legal marriages/yr (declining trend); industry estimate ~80–100k "celebrated" weddings.
- Realistic dedicated wedding-venue universe in SA: **roughly 1,200–2,000 venues** that do weddings as a primary line (farms, estates, guesthouses with function space, lodges). Of those, maybe **400–600 are mid-tier "serious" venues** doing 20+ weddings/year.

**TAM math (upper-bound, fantasy):** 2,000 venues × R1499 × 12 = **R36m ARR**. 100% of mid-tier (500) = **R9m ARR**. Realistic SAM at 30% of mid-tier = **R2.7m ARR**. This is a lifestyle/cashflow business at best, not a venture-scale one.

Regional shape: ~40% Western Cape (Winelands + Overberg + West Coast), ~25% Gauteng, ~15% KZN (South Coast + Midlands), rest scattered.

## 2. Competitors

**Direct SaaS — international, accessible in ZA:**
- **Sonas** (Ireland) — £99/mo + per-event fee. The closest analogue. Wedding-venue specific. ([sonas.events](https://www.sonas.events/), [Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/229022/Sonas/))
- **HoneyBook** — from ~$16–$32/mo. Built for US planners/freelancers, not venue ops, but bleeds into the market. ([honeybook.com](https://www.honeybook.com/venue-management-software))
- **Aisle Planner** — from $39.99/mo. Planner-centric. ([aisleplanner](https://www.eventcertificate.com/aisle-planner-pricing-plans/))
- **Tripleseat / Event Temple / iVvy / Perfect Venue** — hotel + multi-event focus, priced for chains (~$200–$500/mo).

**ZA-built**: effectively none. GetApp/Capterra ZA directories surface only re-listed international tools. No ZA wedding-venue SaaS of any scale found in search.

**Real incumbent workflow (the actual competitor):**
- Excel + WhatsApp + Gmail + a WordPress site. This is what 80%+ of ZA venues run on.
- The on-site coordinator or external wedding planner (Aleit, Wedding Concepts, eHire) holds the spreadsheet.
- Notion for the more tech-curious 5%.

## 3. Buying behaviour

ZA hospitality does buy SaaS — but reluctantly and cheap:

- **Dineplan** (restaurant reservations) charges R2,900/yr (~R240/mo). Processed 4.3m reservations in 2023. That's the price ceiling reference. ([Dineplan pricing](https://restaurants.dineplan.com/pricing))
- **NightsBridge** (B&B/lodge channel manager) is the canonical ZA hospitality SaaS — sub-R500/mo, near-monopoly.

**R1499/mo is high for ZA SMB hospitality.** Dineplan is ~6× cheaper. NightsBridge is ~3× cheaper. Venues will compare to those, not to Sonas's £99.

Distribution channels:
- **Facebook groups** ("SA Wedding Suppliers", "Wedding Planners SA") — real, active, where leads/gossip flow.
- **SA Wedding Expos**, **Wedding Concepts**, **Aleit Group** networks.
- **saweddings.co.za / Pink Book / Confetti Daydreams** directory listings — pay-to-play SEO.
- Cold outreach to GM/owner direct works better than ads (small market, everyone knows everyone).

## 4. Honest verdict

**Would this succeed?** As a venture: no. As a R1–3m ARR solo lifestyle SaaS with a moat in ZA-specific workflows (Rands, per-head menus, ZA tax, Cape-Winelands seasonality): plausibly yes.

**12 / 24 / 36-month revenue:**
- **Best**: 15 / 60 / 150 paying venues → R270k / R1.08m / R2.7m ARR.
- **Base**: 5 / 20 / 50 → R90k / R360k / R900k ARR.
- **Worst**: 1 (Pat Busch) / 3 / 5 → sub-R100k ARR, churn kills it by month 30.

**Biggest single risk:** the wedding planner owns the workflow, not the venue. If Aleit/Wedding Concepts says "we use our own system", venues defer. The couple portal is genuinely novel — but planners will see it as **disintermediation** and actively block it.

**What 10×s it:**
1. **Sell to the planner, not the venue** — white-label the portal so Aleit ships it under their brand. Flip the threat into the channel.
2. **Take payments**. R1499/mo is forgettable; 1% of R150k weddings × 50 venues is R7.5m. Yoco rails are already scaffolded.
3. Expand to **safari lodges + boutique guesthouse functions** — same workflow, 3× the TAM.

**Verdict**: Build it, ship Pat Busch, get 5 paying logos by month 6 on hand-sold relationships, then decide whether to chase the planner channel or kill it. Don't raise money against this.
